# Alembic Quickstart (for educators)

Alembic helps you turn your teaching material into a polished, shareable open
educational resource — and publish it online — without learning any developer
tools. You organize the knowledge; Alembic handles the rest.

You'll go from a blank page to a published study guide with a live web page in
about 15 minutes.

> Want a worked example to follow along? [DemoContent.md](DemoContent.md) is a
> complete "Acid–Base Equilibria" package — copy-paste sections, a concept map,
> AI prompts, and instructor notes — that touches every feature below.

---

## 1. Sign in

Click **Sign in** → **Continue with GitHub**. This just confirms who you are.
Alembic gets no access to your files until you choose to publish later.

## 2. Create a package

A *package* is one teaching resource — a topic, a module, a unit. In your
**Workspace**, give it a title (e.g. "Acid–Base Chemistry"), pick a license
(CC-BY is a good default), and click **Create**. It starts in a private trial
space — no GitHub needed yet.

## 3. Write your study guide

Open the editor. Your guide is a list of **sections**. For each section, write a
heading and your content in plain text. Chemistry and math just work:

- `H~2~O` shows as H₂O, `CO~3~^2-^` as CO₃²⁻
- `$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$` renders as a formula
- `$\ce{2H2 + O2 -> 2H2O}$` renders a balanced equation

The **Preview** on the right updates as you type. Use **↑ ↓** to reorder
sections and **✕** to remove one. Click **Save** when you're happy.

## 4. Let AI help (optional)

- **✨ Draft a section with AI** — describe what you want ("Explain Le
  Chatelier's principle with an everyday example"), review the draft, and
  **Add to study guide** or discard it. You're always in control.
- **Generate worksheet** — tick the sections you want covered and Alembic
  drafts a student worksheet from them. Open it with **View**. If you later
  change those sections, the worksheet is flagged out-of-date so you can
  **Regenerate** or **Keep mine**.

## 5. Share a self-contained file

**Download .md.html** gives you a single file that opens in any browser *and*
keeps your editable source inside it — handy for emailing a colleague or
keeping an offline copy.

## 6. Publish to the web

When you're ready to make it public:

1. **Connect publishing** (one-time) — connect your GitHub account so Alembic
   can store and publish your materials. Your private notes always stay
   private, in a separate place students never see.
2. **Publish to GitHub** — saves your package online.
3. **Publish website** — creates a real web page for your study guide. After a
   minute you'll get a link like `https://you.github.io/your-package/` to share
   with students. **Preview student page** shows you exactly what they'll see
   first.

## 7. List it for others to find

**List on index** adds your package to Alembic's public **Discover** page so
other educators can find, view, and adapt it.

---

## Good to know

- **Nothing is lost.** Every save is a version you can go back to — use
  **Restore** under Publishing.
- **Public vs private is real.** Instructor notes and answer keys live in a
  separate private space, never in the public web page or its history.
- **It's yours.** Your package is stored in your own GitHub account and stays
  usable even without Alembic.
